INTRO

A coffee shop is a retail establishment that primarily serves coffee, tea, and other hot beverages, often accompanied by pastries, sandwiches, or light snacks. It typically provides a comfortable atmosphere for socializing or working and may offer free Wi-Fi access. Historically, coffeehouses were known as cafés and served only coffee.

Cappucino

A cappuccino is a hot coffee drink that is made of equal proportions of espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. The drink is typically served in a small cup and is characterized by its rich and creamy texture, as well as its distinct three-layered appearance.

Black Coffee

Black coffee is simply coffee that is normally brewed without the addition of additives such as sugar, milk, cream, or added flavors. While it has a slightly bitter taste compared to when it is flavored with additives, many people love a strong cup of black coffee. In fact, for some, it is part of their everyday diet.

Black Coffee

A dry cappuccino has the basic layers of the standard cup but with different proportions. Instead of having equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foamed milk, a dry cappuccino has very little steamed milk and a lot more foam. This is why it’s called dry as the foam is the least dense (liquid) portion of the drink.

About

The third wave coffee movement, which originated in the United States in the early 2000s, emphasizes high-quality, artisanal coffee made with precision and care. Third wave cafés focus on sourcing specialty beans from specific regions, roasting them in-house, and brewing them using methods such as pour-over, AeroPress, or siphon. These cafés often have minimalist, industrial-chic interiors and a menu that highlights single-origin coffees, espresso-based drinks, and alternative milk options. Beyond coffee, third wave cafés may also offer gourmet pastries, small-batch teas, and locally sourced snacks, catering to discerning coffee connoisseurs and food enthusiasts alike.
